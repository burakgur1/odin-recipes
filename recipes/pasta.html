Pasta Carbonara

Description

Pasta Carbonara is a classic Roman dish that combines simple ingredients into a creamy, luxurious pasta. With just eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper, this elegant dish proves that the best meals don't require complicated preparations.

The magic of carbonara lies in the emulsion created when hot pasta water, eggs, and cheese come together, forming a silky sauce that coats every strand of pasta. This authentic Italian recipe is quick to prepare and absolutely irresistible.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound spaghetti or fettuccine
  • 6 ounces pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 teaspoons freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook pasta until al dente.
  2. While pasta cooks, whisk together eggs in a bowl with Pecorino Romano, Parmesan, and black pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add diced pancetta and cook until crispy, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining the pasta.
  6. Add hot pasta to the skillet with pancetta, tossing to combine.
  7. Remove skillet from heat.
  8. Slowly pour egg mixture over pasta while tossing constantly.
  9. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time until you reach desired sauce consistency.
  10. Toss everything together until the sauce is creamy and coats the pasta.
  11. Serve immediately with extra black pepper and Pecorino Romano cheese.
